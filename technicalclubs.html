ACM Manipal

ACM Manipal is the official student chapter of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) for Manipal University. ACM is the world's largest scientific and educational computing society. ACM ICPC (also referred to as the “Olympics of Programming Competitions”), and the A.M. Turing Awards, are two of ACM’s most prominent events.

ACM Manipal, is very different from any other technical club on campus because we have a flat hierarchy in place. That means that our Core Committee consists of students from all years across all branches. We believe that age cannot be considered a factor to determine one’s competency.

IECSE Manipal

Established in 2007, IECSE is the official club of the Department of CSE, MIT Manipal. Aimed to promote technical awareness amongst the college students, IECSE provides a platform for students to hone their technical skills irrespective of their field of study. One of the largest technical clubs in Manipal, it is a community of tech enthusiasts with support of its alumni members. Every odd semester, few students from I/II Year are selected as working/management committee members to handle the club activities.

The club organizes weekly workshops for programming, designing & development and is known to hold regular contests for coders & nerds alike. The annual fest Prometheus brings all the geeky minds together in a variety of competitions. IECSE boasts of huge international participation in its bi-annual scavenger hunt HawkEye/CyberHawk. Other events include PSUC/DS/OOP Workshops and Placement Talk which are held to guide the students with their academic curriculum. The club members have proved their mettle regularly in Microsoft's Code.Fun.Do., Google Summer of Code, AngelHack, ICPC and numerous other campus/national events.

The Astronomy Club

The Astronomy Club is a student-run pure sciences club that organizes talks, workshops and sky-watches. Geeks and bookworms, we love holding forth on all things astronomy - from particles to planets, from quarks to black holes - and, as engineers, we tinker around on projects such as a star tracker and a radio-telescope. Our flagship events are the all-night sky-watches organised at different locations around the MIT campus, such as Hiriadka and Kundadri peak at Agumbe.
